# Cardiovascular Patient Outcomes & Hospital Operations Analytics

## Project Overview

This healthcare analytics project explores the relationship between cardiovascular patient outcomes, hospital admissions, mortality risk, and environmental air quality factors using real-world hospital and pollution datasets from Hisar, Haryana (2017–2019).

The objective was to identify operational trends, disease-specific mortality risks, and the potential impact of air quality on healthcare demand while developing an interactive analytics dashboard for decision-making.

## Business Problem

Healthcare administrators need to understand:

* Which cardiovascular conditions carry the highest mortality risk.
* How hospital admissions vary over time.
* Whether poor air quality increases healthcare demand.
* Key operational indicators affecting hospital performance.

This project transforms raw hospital and environmental data into actionable insights through data analysis and dashboarding.

## Dataset

* Cardiovascular patient admission records
* Mortality and discharge outcomes
* Air Quality Index (AQI) data
* Environmental pollutant measurements
* Weather indicators

Location: Hisar, Haryana, India

Period: 2017–2019

## Key Insights

### Disease Mortality Risk

Highest mortality rates were observed among:

1. Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) – 16.2%
2. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) – 14.4%
3. Heart Failure – 14.1%

These conditions represent the highest-risk patient groups and may require targeted clinical interventions.

### Air Quality Impact

Patients admitted during poorer air-quality conditions showed slightly higher admission volumes compared to days with better air quality.

### Operational Trends

* Admissions increased significantly from 2017 to 2018.
* Peak admission volumes were observed during late-year months.
* Mortality events remained relatively infrequent compared to total admissions.

### Healthcare Resource Planning

The dashboard enables healthcare administrators to:

* Monitor admission trends.
* Identify high-risk disease groups.
* Track environmental health impacts.
* Support resource allocation and capacity planning.
